**Translate PDF Online** helps researchers and teams translate **PDF documents** between **English, Chinese (Simplified), and Spanish** while preserving columns, tables, figures, and typography where the pipeline allows.

## What you can do

- **Upload a PDF**, choose source and target languages, and **download a translated PDF**.  
- Use **credits** (e.g. per translated page) or **subscription** plans; pay securely via **Creem**.  
- Preview and compare originals and translations in the browser when the product UI exposes those features.

## How it works (high level)

Our stack combines a **Next.js** front end (hosted on **Cloudflare** edge), **R2** storage for files, backend APIs, asynchronous workers for layout analysis and rendering, and **DeepSeek** (and related tooling) for translation. The pipeline builds on open-source work such as **[zotero-pdf2zh](https://github.com/guaguastandup/zotero-pdf2zh)** and the **BabelDOC / pdf2zh** ecosystem.

More detail, architecture diagrams (text), and **AGPL** transparency are in the **[Documentation](/docs)**.

## Privacy and retention

Uploaded and generated files in object storage are scheduled for **automatic deletion after about seven days**, as described in our **[Privacy Policy](/privacy-policy)**. Please do not upload documents you are not allowed to process.

## Get started

1. Open **[www.translatepdfonline.com](https://www.translatepdfonline.com)**.  
2. Sign in and go to **Translate** (or your locale path).  
3. Upload a PDF, pick languages, and start a job.  
4. Buy credits or subscribe on **[Pricing](/pricing)** if needed.
